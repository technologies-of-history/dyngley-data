And stampe hem wel togidir with fresshe swyne grece and put therto reclis a litel hony & vyrgyn waxe & whan all these thynges ben stamped wel. gader hem into a clene bason or panne & do þerto white wyne & lete it stonde so al a day & al a nyght. and on that other day in the morowe sette it on a fire & sethe it wel & ȝeve it good walmes and whan it is wel sode take it fro the fyre & holde it in to a poke maad of strong canevas & so streyne it oute in to a vessel & put it in to a boxe & as longe as the heed is soor a- noynte it therwith & than it wol hele faire & wel.For broken hedes of eny wepen falle or hurte.If the sculle or brayn panne be broke or hurte so that the pacient may not speke stampe vyolet & ȝeve it hym to drinke in wyne and if the right side of the heed be hurte. stampe violet & bynde it to the sool of the lifte foot and if it be the lifte side ley it to the sool of the right foot. And than the boon of the heed that is hurte wol arise up
